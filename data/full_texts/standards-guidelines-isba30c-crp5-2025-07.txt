Explanatory note
1. The draft regulations on the exploitation of mineral resources in the Area (ISBA/25/C/WP.1) anticipate
the development of specific standards and guidelines to support their implementation. The standards will be
mandatory for both Contractors and the International Seabed Authority, while the guidelines will serve as non-
binding recommendations (ISBA/25/C/19/Add.1, section IV.A and Annex). The Legal and Technical
Commission has recommended, and the Council has endorsed, an outcome-based approach for their
development, with the standards and guidelines to be implemented in phases as follows:
• Phase 1: Standards and guidelines deemed necessary to be in place by the time of adoption of the draft
regulations on exploitation.
• Phase 2: Standards and guidelines deemed necessary to be in place before the receipt of an application of
a plan of work for exploitation.
• Phase 3: Standards and guidelines deemed necessary to be in place before commercial mining activities
commence in the Area.
2. This paper presents a list of standards and guidelines designed to support the implementation of the draft
regulations on the exploitation of mineral resources in the Area. The list includes Phase 1 standards and
guidelines that were prepared by the Legal and Technical Commission and released for the Council’s
consideration in 2022, which are indicated in bold. Additionally, it outlines anticipated standards and guidelines
based on the ongoing negotiations of the Revised Consolidated Text (ISBA/30/C/CRP.1) and the Revised
Suspense Document (ISBA/30/C/CRP.2), which are presented in italics.
3. For further guidance on the development of standards and guidelines, reference is made to the
Secretariat’s note on the proposed content and development of standards and guidelines1, as well as the Pretoria
Workshop Report2. Regarding the placement and hierarchy of the respective standards and guidelines, a proposal
has been put forward in Annex-I_-placement-hierarchy-criteria-.pdf. The current list provides a snapshot of the
ongoing negotiations and will be updated as discussions on the regulations progress. The final decision on
whether to include potential standards and guidelines is yet to be made. Therefore, the list below should be
considered solely as a reference tool to guide discussions.
